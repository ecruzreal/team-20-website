Our Research

This research study was conducted to better understand the behaviors, challenges, and expectations of personal health finance management and AI-assisted planning tools. There were 17 responses from the initial survey to gage the overall perception of health finances. Some of the key findings from what individuals struggle with include budgeting inconsistencies, investment making decisions, tax-related confusion, and the overall lack of understanding/financial guidance. It is key to note that there were very mixed feelings of AI-assistance for financial health planning due to various reasons including data security, reliability, and trust. Based on the survey and the specific answers, it is in the best interest of the project to move forward with high emphasis on prioritizing transparency, personalized insights, financial forecasting tools, and simplified decision support.

Online survey was the primary method with a total of 17 responses. The primary targets were college students and young adults. Anyone from this age range who are or want to use some type of digital financial tools. The survey included questions revolving around current financial tools being used, financial confusion, desired features in a new AI-based application or tool, etc.

Most of the participants from the study use banking apps such as Chase or Bank of America. They also use investing platforms like Robinhood or Charles Schwab. Budgeting tools are not as common, but tools like Rocket Money is something that has been used by some individuals. In general, there are two main categories that the participants are categorized in, and that being those who are active investors and those that have budgeting issues. Some are focused on maximizing their money and minimizing costs, while others are simply seeking general guidance of budgeting.

There are 4 major findings from the survey responses. These include budgeting/expense tracking, investment decision making, the overall lack of guidance from current existing tools, and hesitation when it comes to only relying on AI-assistance.

  • Budgeting/expense tracking:

    Lots of issues with tracking subscriptions, managing recurring payments, basic understanding of how much to save from paychecks, and avoiding overspending.

  • Investment decision making:

    Confusion around when to buy or sell assets/investments, long-term financial projections, managing funds and optimization strategies

  • Overall lack of guidance from current existing tools:

    Lack of clarity from an informational guidance perspective, minimal proactive support, and current tools are not very easy to use

  • Hesitation when it comes to only relying on AI-assistance:

    Concerns about security issues, biased recommendations, lack of reliability, wanting some type of proven track record, believes that it can be useful if more trust in involved

User Needs and Features
